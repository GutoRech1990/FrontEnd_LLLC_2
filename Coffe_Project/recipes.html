Coffee Recipes

1. Classic Espresso

Espresso is the base for many other coffee recipes and is loved for its intensity and concentrated flavor. This is a classic drink, popular in coffee shops around the world.

Ingredients:

  • 7 to 9 grams of ground coffee (preferably Arabica beans)
  • Filtered water

Instructions:

  1. Warm the cup in which the coffee will be served.
  2. Place the ground coffee in the espresso machine's portafilter and lightly tamp it down.
  3. Set the machine to extract 30 ml of coffee, which should be extracted in about 25 seconds.
  4. Serve the espresso immediately, enjoying its creaminess and intensity.

Tip: If you don't have an espresso machine at home, you can use a stovetop espresso maker for a strong and full-bodied drink.

2. Homemade Cappuccino

Cappuccino is a perfect combination of coffee, steamed milk, and milk foam. It has a balanced flavor between the bitterness of coffee and the smoothness of milk, ideal for those who prefer a creamy drink.

Ingredients:

  • 1 shot of espresso (30 ml)
  • 100 ml of whole milk
  • Chocolate powder or cinnamon for sprinkling (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the espresso following the previous recipe.
  2. Heat the milk in a pitcher or microwave until it begins to steam (do not boil).
  3. Use a steamer or hand frother to froth the milk. If you don’t have a frother, you can shake the milk in a glass jar with a lid until it foams.
  4. Pour the coffee into a cup, then add the steamed milk, holding back the foam with a spoon so the liquid milk goes in first.
  5. Top the coffee with the milk foam.
  6. Sprinkle with chocolate powder or cinnamon, if desired.

Tip: The classic cappuccino ratio is 1/3 espresso, 1/3 milk, and 1/3 foam.

3. Chocolate Mocha

Mocha is a delicious variation that combines the rich taste of coffee with the sweetness of chocolate. It's perfect for those who enjoy a sweeter drink.

Ingredients:

  • 1 shot of espresso (30 ml)
  • 150 ml of milk
  • 2 tablespoons of chocolate powder or chocolate syrup
  • Whipped cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a cup, add the chocolate powder or chocolate syrup.
  2. Prepare the espresso and pour it over the chocolate, stirring well to dissolve the chocolate completely in the coffee.
  3. Heat the milk and froth it using a steamer, hand frother, or by shaking it in a jar.
  4. Pour the steamed milk over the coffee and chocolate mixture.
  5. Top with whipped cream and, if desired, sprinkle a bit of chocolate on top.

Tip: For an extra special touch, add a dash of cinnamon or nutmeg on top.

4. Iced Coffee with Condensed Milk (Cà Phê Sữa Đá)

This recipe is inspired by the famous Vietnamese coffee that combines strong coffee with condensed milk and is served cold. It's a refreshing and sweet drink, perfect for hot days.

Ingredients:

  • 100 ml of brewed coffee or strong espresso
  • 2 tablespoons of condensed milk
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. Put the condensed milk at the bottom of a tall glass.
  2. Pour the hot coffee over the condensed milk and stir well until they are combined.
  3. Add ice to the mixture.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy the refreshing taste and sweetness of condensed milk combined with coffee.

Tip: Adjust the amount of condensed milk based on your preference for a sweeter or more balanced drink.

5. Affogato

Affogato is a simple yet incredibly delicious Italian dessert. It combines the intensity of coffee with the creaminess of ice cream, creating a unique flavor experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 shot of espresso (30 ml)
  • 1 scoop of vanilla ice cream

Instructions:

  1. Place a generous scoop of vanilla ice cream in a small cup or glass.
  2. Slowly pour the hot espresso over the ice cream.
  3. Serve immediately and enjoy the melted ice cream mixed with coffee.

Tip: For an interesting twist, you can substitute vanilla ice cream with chocolate or caramel ice cream.
